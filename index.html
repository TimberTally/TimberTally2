Simplified Tree ID Key (Eastern US Deciduous)

Note: This is a simplified guide. Always confirm with a field guide.

1.

  • a. Leaves/buds Opposite → Go to 2
  • b. Leaves/buds Alternate → Go to 4

2. (Opposite Arrangement)

  • a. Leaves Compound. Stout twigs, large leaf scars. → Ash (Fraxinus spp.)
  • b. Leaves Simple. → Go to 3

3. (Opposite, Simple Leaves)

  • a. Leaves Lobed. Fruit: paired winged samara. → Maple (Acer spp.)
  • b. Leaves Unlobed. Blocky bark. Fruit: drupe cluster. → Flowering Dogwood (Cornus florida)

4. (Alternate Arrangement)

  • a. Leaves Compound. → Go to 5
  • b. Leaves Simple. → Go to 6

5. (Alternate, Compound Leaves)

  • a. Pith Chambered. Large leaf scars. → Walnut (Juglans spp.)
  • b. Pith Solid. Stout twigs. → Hickory (Carya spp.)
  • c. Often thorny. Numerous small leaflets. → Locust (Robinia/Gleditsia spp.)

6.

  • a. Leaves Lobed. → Go to 7
  • b. Leaves Unlobed. → Go to 8

7. (Alternate, Simple, Lobed)

  • a. Lobes Rounded. Fruit: Acorn. → White Oak Group
  • b. Lobes Pointed w/ Bristle Tip. Fruit: Acorn. → Red Oak Group
  • c. Variable shapes. Aromatic (spicy). → Sassafras
  • d. 4 Lobes, tip Flattened. Tulip-shaped flower. → Tulip Poplar

8. (Alternate, Simple, Unlobed)

  • a. Margin Entire. → Go to 9
  • b. Margin Toothed. → Go to 10

9.

  • a. Bark smooth, gray, Muscle-like. Buds long, slender. → American Beech
  • b. Bark dark, Blocky. Leaves thick/glossy. → Black Gum

10.

  • a. Bark Peeling horizontally. Horizontal lenticels. → Birch
  • b. Bark NOT peeling. → Go to 11

11.

  • a. Leaf base Asymmetrical. Surface often Rough. → Elm
  • b. Leaf base Symmetrical. → Go to 12

12.

  • a. Cherry/almond smell from twig. Small fruit. → Cherry
  • b. Leaf Triangular/Heart-shaped. Petiole Flattened. → Poplar / Aspen
  • c. Finely Toothed. Bark smooth, gray, muscle-like. Small tree. → American Hornbeam
  • d. Doubly Serrate. Bark grayish, Shreddy. Hop-like fruit. → Eastern Hophornbeam
